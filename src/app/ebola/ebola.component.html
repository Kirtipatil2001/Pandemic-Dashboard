How Ebola has Started?

Since its discovery in 1976, the majority of cases and outbreaks of Ebola Virus Diseases have occurred in Africa. The 2014-2016 Ebola outbreak in /west Africa began in a rural setting of southeastern Guinea, spread to urban areas and across borders within weeks, and became a global epidemic within months.

Effect of Ebola

Aside from the devastating health effects, the Ebola epidemic also had a pronounced socio-economic impact in Guinea, Liberia,and Sierra Leone. According to 2014 projections from the World Bank, an estimated $2.2 billion was lost in 2015 in the gross domestic product of the three countries.

The long-term impact of these disruptions to deep-seated human traditions is not yet known. Psychologists are concerned that suspicions that other people may harbour the deadly virus will take some time to melt away.
